# ✅ Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India
---

> **Report cybercrime at:** 🌐 [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
> **Helpline:** 📞 **1930** (National Cyber Crime Helpline)

---

## 🔐 Account & Password Security

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., `C@mpus#2024!`). Never reuse your college email password elsewhere.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, Instagram, WhatsApp, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorised access.

- [ ] **3. Never share OTPs with anyone** — no bank, government body, or UPI app will ever call you and ask for an OTP. Sharing it even with a "customer care" caller is a scam.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety *(Mandatory for all students)*

- [ ] **4. 🏦 Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking or UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm). Keep it low (e.g., ₹2,000–₹5,000/day) and raise it only when needed.

- [ ] **5. 🏦 Never scan a QR code to "receive" money** — QR codes are for making payments, not receiving them. Scammers send fake QR codes claiming to send you money, but they actually debit your account.

- [ ] **6. Verify before you pay** — always double-check the recipient's UPI ID or phone number before confirming any transaction, especially for marketplace purchases (OLX, Facebook Marketplace).

---

## 🌐 Browsing & Social Media Safety

- [ ] **7. Check for HTTPS before entering any personal data** — look for the padlock icon in the browser address bar. Never enter your bank details or login credentials on HTTP sites.

- [ ] **8. Avoid using public Wi-Fi (college canteen, café, metro) for banking or logging into sensitive accounts** — use your mobile data instead, or install a trusted VPN.

- [ ] **9. Limit personal information on social media** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, Aadhaar details, college ID, or daily schedule publicly. Cybercriminals use this for identity theft and targeted scams.

---

## 📧 Phishing & Scam Awareness

- [ ] **10. Think before you click** — do not click links in unsolicited emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS claiming you've "won a prize," "received a scholarship," or "need to verify your account." Go directly to the official website instead.

- [ ] **11. Verify job offers independently** — fake internship and part-time job scams are common on Telegram and Instagram. Always verify by calling the company on their official number listed on their website.

---

## 🛡️ Device & Data Protection

- [ ] **12. Keep your phone and apps updated** — software updates often patch security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit. Enable auto-updates for your OS and banking apps.

---

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim – Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (call anytime) |
| 🏦 **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Found on the back of your debit/credit card |
| 🚔 **Local Cyber Police Station** | File an FIR for serious offences |

> ⏱️ **Act fast** — report financial fraud within the first 30 minutes for the best chance of freezing the transaction and recovering money.
