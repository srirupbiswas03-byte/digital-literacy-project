# Project Report – Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness
## Part B: Personal Reflection (Q.26)

---

### What Surprised Me Most While Researching This Topic

While researching cybercrime, what surprised me most was how alarmingly ordinary the victims are. I had assumed cybercrime largely targeted the careless or technically illiterate — but the data tells a very different story. Educated college students, working professionals, and even IT-literate individuals fall victim to UPI scams and phishing attacks every day in India. The "receive money via QR code" scam, in particular, was a revelation — its logic is deliberately designed to exploit trust and create urgency. I was also struck by how quickly financial fraud can occur; in some cases, a victim's account is drained within minutes of one wrong tap. India's rising digital payments adoption, while hugely positive, has also created a vast new attack surface that many young users are unprepared for.

---

### One Habit I Will Personally Change

After this research, I will immediately set a daily UPI transaction limit on my phone. I had never considered doing this before — it felt unnecessary. But understanding how fast fraud transactions happen, and how a single moment of distraction can lead to major financial loss, has convinced me this is a simple, high-impact step. It costs nothing, takes two minutes to set up, and limits the worst-case damage of any scam significantly.
