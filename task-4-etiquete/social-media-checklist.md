# 📱 Social Media Do's and Don'ts
### Responsible Use for College Students

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This | Why It Matters |
|---|---------|----------------|
| 1 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" | Your digital footprint is permanent; even deleted posts can be screenshotted and shared. |
| 2 | **Keep your privacy settings updated** — review them every few months on Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn. | Default settings are often public; controlling your audience protects your personal safety. |
| 3 | **Use LinkedIn actively and professionally** — post about projects, certifications, and achievements. | Recruiters actively search LinkedIn; a strong profile can lead directly to internship offers. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing others' work** — tag the original creator or source when reposting content. | It's ethical, legally important, and builds your reputation as a trustworthy online presence. |
| 5 | **Engage respectfully in disagreements** — use facts and calm language when debating opinions online. | Thoughtful discourse builds credibility; aggressive responses can go viral for the wrong reasons. |
| 6 | **Log out of shared or college lab computers** after using any social media or email account. | Failing to log out can allow others to post as you or access your private messages. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This | Why It's Harmful |
|---|------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Don't post confidential college information** — exam papers, internal notices, faculty communications, or classmates' personal details. | This can lead to disciplinary action, breach of trust, or even legal consequences under IT laws. |
| 2 | **Don't overshare your location in real time** — avoid posting "live" check-ins, hostel room details, or daily routines publicly. | Real-time location sharing is a safety risk, especially for students living away from home. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — neither participate in trolling, pile-ons, or harassment, nor stay silent when you witness it. | Cyberbullying causes serious psychological harm; bystander silence enables it to continue. |
| 4 | **Don't post in anger or react impulsively** — avoid responding to provocative posts immediately; wait at least 10 minutes. | Impulsive posts made in frustration are frequently regretted and can damage relationships permanently. |
| 5 | **Don't use your professional accounts (LinkedIn, college email) casually** — avoid memes, complaints about college, or informal language. | Mixing personal and professional tones confuses your audience and undermines your credibility. |
| 6 | **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers** without verifying the account is genuine. | Fake profiles are used for phishing, catfishing, identity theft, and targeted scams. |

---

## 🧭 Quick Reference Card

```
PAUSE → THINK → POST
    ↓
Is it True?  Is it Kind?  Is it Necessary?
    ↓
If not all three → reconsider before sharing.
```
