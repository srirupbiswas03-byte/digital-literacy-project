### A Situation Where Poor Digital Communication Caused a Real Problem

In 2013, Justine Sacco — a PR professional with around 170 Twitter followers — posted a poorly worded, tone-deaf joke about Africa and AIDS just before boarding a long-haul flight. By the time her plane landed roughly eleven hours later, the tweet had been shared thousands of times worldwide. The hashtag #HasJustineLandedYet trended globally as users waited in real time for her to discover what had happened. She was fired from her job before she even switched her phone back on.

What went wrong was a failure of basic digital judgment: she posted impulsively, misjudged her audience entirely, and treated a public platform as if it were a private conversation with close friends.

What she should have done differently is simple — pause, reread, and ask: "How will a complete stranger interpret this, stripped of all context and tone?" A ten-second check would have prevented a career-ending, globally viral moment. This case is a stark reminder that on social media, there is no such thing as a small or invisible audience.
